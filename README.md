# Predict whether an employee within an organization should receive a promotion.

# Introduction

The client, a large multinational corporation (MNC), operates across 15 broad verticals. One significant challenge the client faces is identifying suitable candidates for promotion, specifically for managerial positions and below, and preparing them in a timely manner. The current process involves several steps:

1. A set of employees is initially identified based on recommendations and past performance.

2. The selected employees undergo separate training and evaluation programs tailored to each vertical. These programs are designed to address the specific skills required for each vertical.

3. At the conclusion of the program, employees are evaluated on various factors, including training performance, to determine their eligibility for promotion.

# Objectives

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a critical phase in machine learning projects, involving the comprehensive exploration and preparation of data before modeling. Summary statistics such as mean, median, mode, and standard deviation provide initial insights into the central tendency and variability of the dataset. These statistics are pivotal in understanding the distributional characteristics of variables, thereby informing subsequent modeling decisions. Visualization techniques, including histograms, box plots, scatter plots, and heatmaps, offer intuitive representations that elucidate data patterns, relationships between variables, and potential outliers. Such visual explorations are essential for uncovering underlying trends and anomalies that may impact model performance.

Handling missing data is another imperative aspect of EDA, involving strategies like imputation or removal of incomplete records to ensure data integrity. Feature engineering plays a pivotal role in enhancing predictive capabilities by transforming existing variables through techniques such as normalization or scaling. 

This process not only optimizes model performance but also facilitates dimensionality reduction when exploring correlations between variables. Correlation analysis further elucidates relationships among features, guiding the selection of relevant predictors for modeling purposes. Moreover, data cleaning procedures ensure the dataset is devoid of duplicates, inconsistencies, and formatting errors, thereby priming it for robust predictive modeling. By integrating these methodologies, EDA serves as a foundational step in the machine learning pipeline, equipping practitioners with the requisite insights to construct accurate and reliable predictive models.

In this scenario, two datasets, the training and testing sets, were employed to perform predictions and assess key metrics including accuracy, F1-score, recall, and precision.
